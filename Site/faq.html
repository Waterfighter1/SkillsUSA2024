Frequently Asked Questions

Due to limited space, we are unable to take in every animal that comes to us. For this reason, we can only take in animals from within city limits. If an animal is found within city limits, an affidavit must be signed stating that the animal was found within city limits. At this time, we are only able to accept cats orphaned or with a critical injury. Any person in Wilson County outside of city limits may call animal control at 615-444-9775. New Leash on Life may be able to help some cats and dogs. They can be reached at 615-444-1144. County K-9 in Lebanon may be able to help cats but they mainly deal with dogs.

We are NOT a no-kill shelter. We do our best to adopt out animals but we cannot and will not guarantee the adoption of any animal.

We are open to the public Wednesday from 11:30am until 3:30pm.

We are open to the public Wednesday from 11:30am until 3:30pm. Otherwise, we are available by appointment as-needed.

We are able to perform animala welfare checks within city limits.

You must have photo ID and proof of residency within city limits to turn in a dog. If this is an Owner surrender, there is a 25 dollar fee payable by cash or check.

Due to our limited capacity, we do not prioritize cats since there is no state law preventing them from roaming at large.

Any dog bites or aggressive dogs will be handled right away as a top priority.
